🌍 Chad Civil War: Humanitarian Crisis Overview & Digital Response

Since 2021, Chad has been gripped by a brutal civil war following the death of President Idriss Déby. Rebel groups and government forces have clashed in key regions including the Lake Chad basin, the Tibesti Mountains, and southern provinces.

The war has devastated civilian life. Entire communities have been uprooted, healthcare infrastructure destroyed, and food and water sources disrupted. In the shadows of airstrikes and gunfire, countless families have fled their homes, facing the horrors of displacement, malnutrition, and insecurity.

📉 The Human Cost:

🧍‍♂️ 380,000 internally displaced people (IDPs) within Chad. [UNHCR, 2024]
🥣 2.1 million facing acute food insecurity. [WFP, 2024]
🏥 1/3 of health facilities are damaged or non-functional. [MSF, 2023]
📚 30% of children are out of school, especially in conflict zones.
🚱 80% of rural residents lack access to safe drinking water.

📲 In such a crisis, hope lies in innovation. A lightweight, intelligent humanitarian aid platform—like ScanCraft—could support real-time data tracking, logistics coordination, survivor registration, and secure communication. In warzones, information saves lives.
